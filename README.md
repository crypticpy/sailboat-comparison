# Bluewater Cruiser Comparison — Patagonia to Polynesia

An interactive, single-page comparison of **27 serious offshore monohulls**, scored against one
specific cruising mission:

> A two-handed couple (one smaller / less-strong crew) plus two dogs, wanting a strongly
> **protected cockpit**, **easy short-handed handling**, a real **workshop**, easy engine access,
> abundant storage and **ocean-crossing range** — for a route from cold **Patagonia & Chile** to
> the tropical **Caribbean, BVI and French Polynesia**. Budget: **under \$1M** fully outfitted.

## View it

Open **`index.html`** in any web browser — no build step, no dependencies, fully self-contained
(works offline). If GitHub Pages is enabled, it's also live at the Pages URL.

## Features

- **Tune your priorities** — drag sliders to re-weight the nine scoring factors; mission-fit scores
  and the whole ranking update live.
- **Compare mode** — add up to four boats to a side-by-side table (specs + all nine scores).
- **Overview chart** — price-vs-mission-fit scatter with a $1M budget line; click a dot to open it.
- **Filters** — by type (aluminium expedition / centre-cockpit / deck-saloon & pilothouse /
  aft-cockpit), hull material, lifting keel, hard shelter, budget fit, a max-entry-price slider, and
  free-text search; plus a **★ favourites** shortlist saved in your browser.
- **Per-boat profiles** — radar chart of the nine scores, pros/cons written for this mission, full
  specs (incl. air draft, engine & drive, cabins, and computed comfort ratio & capsize screen),
  price detail with specific brokerage examples, fun facts, **sources & further reading**, and
  curated **YouTube** owner/review links (80+ across the fleet).
- **Colour-scored stats** — a traffic-light (green/yellow/orange/red) **Performance & safety scorecard** in every profile, plus colour-coded values in the spec sheet, rating comfort ratio, capsize screen, ballast ratio, SA/D, D/L, hull speed, fuel/water range and draft against bluewater rules of thumb.
- **Awards & expert take** — sourced endorsements per boat from respected captains, authors and cruising YouTubers (John Harries / Attainable Adventure Cruising, Jimmy Cornell, Sailing Zingaro, SV Delos, the European Yacht of the Year & Cruising World Boat of the Year juries, and more), with award badges on the cards.
- **Selection score** — the headline number is now a tunable weighted average of four pillars: **Mission fit**, **Seaworthiness** (motion comfort, stability, protected helm, offshore toughness), **Ownership & support** (maintainability, simplicity, parts, community) and **Value** (price + budget fit). A second slider panel re-blends the pillars; the default weighting reflects an experienced skipper sailing with a first-time crew.
- **Ownership, support & maintainability** — every profile now has a colour-coded support scorecard (owner-maintainability, systems simplicity, parts & builder support, community strength), plus builder status, systems complexity, mast step, keel attachment & rudder type, known-problem history, real owner-sentiment, and **linked owner communities/forums** — with **risk banners and card flags** where parts or community support are weak.
- **Captain's panel** — a cross-fleet review (channeling the cited captains' documented philosophies) on simplicity for an inexperienced crew, builder/parts continuity, community depth, and rig/keel/rudder technicals.
- **Cards or sortable table** views.

## How the "mission fit" score works

Each boat is rated 1–5 on nine factors, then weighted. The defaults weight cockpit protection ×1.5
and short-handed ease ×1.5 the most, high-latitude capability ×1.25, tropical comfort ×0.75, and
dog-friendliness, workshop/engine access, storage, tankage/range and budget fit each ×1.0 — all
adjustable with the priority sliders. Comfort ratio and capsize screening formula are computed from
each boat's displacement, beam and waterline length.

## Caveat

Scores are editorial judgements synthesised from builder data, magazine reviews (Yachting World,
SAIL, Cruising World, Yachting Monthly, Practical Sailor), SailboatData, the Attainable Adventure
Cruising archive and live brokerage listings — a starting point for your own sea-trials and surveys,
not gospel. Prices are approximate USD ranges as of mid-2026 and move with the market and exchange
rates. Always confirm specs, draft options and outfitting on the specific hull you're considering.
